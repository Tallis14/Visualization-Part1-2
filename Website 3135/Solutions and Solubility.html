Solutions and Solubility

Basics

1. Mixtures are easily separated and often mixed unevenly.
2. Mixtures can be separated by sifting, filtration, magnetism, and sedimentation (letting it sit until it separates).
3. Solutions are homogeneous (evenly mixed).
4.Solutions are made by dissolving one substance into another.
5. Solutes are what is being dissolved (salt or sugar). Solvents are the liquid or gas doing the dissolving (water). Solution is made when these two things are combined.
6. Solubility describes the ability of substances to form a solution. If you are combining two liquids and they form a solution, this is considered miscible. If two liquids do not form     a solution, this is considered immiscible.
7. The more solute that is dissolved, the higher the concentration of the solution. The less solvent used to dissolve the solute, the higher the concentration of the solution.
